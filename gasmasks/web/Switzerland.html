  • Refrence: SZ-001

  • Notes: This early Swiss civilian gas mask uses a design similar to the German VM37.   • Type:GM-33
  • Translation:
  • Aliases: n/a
  • Date: c. 1930s
  • Frequency: Rare
  • Country: Switzerland
  • Use: Military
  • Refrence: SZ-002
  • Price:? $100+
  • Notes: The Swiss GM-33 was the primary military gas mask of the Swiss Army during WWII. It features a long corrugated breathing tube attached to a heavyweight filter canister. The unit was carried in a canvas sack and worn at the wearer's side. The face piece design is very similar to the civilian C-Mask, with identical eye pieces and a similar head harness. The Swiss GM-33 can still be found today in excellent condition, but is getting more rare.
  • Type:C-Mask
  • Translation:
  • Aliases: n/a
  • Date: c. 1930s/1940s
  • Frequency: Uncommon
  • Country: Switzerland
  • Use: Civilian
  • Refrence: SZ-003
  • Price: $60
  • Notes: The Swiss C-Mask is the most commonly found Swiss mask from the WWII era. It was issued in a tall metal canister and came with a 40mm thread filter canister. An outlet valve is positioned on the front of the mask and an adjustable elastic head harness is used for suspension. The mask's face piece was made of green rubber, but black and fabricized versions were also made and are much less common. The C-Mask coupled with a different filter was also issued to fire fighters.

  • Type:Pferde-Gasmaske (Horse Gas Mask)

  • Frequency: Rare
  • Country: Switzerland
  • Use: Military
  • Refrence: SZ-006
  • Price:? $150+
  • Notes: This interesting mask is made for horses. The mask was fitted over the horse's snout and the large filter canister was strapped to the horse's side.   • Type:SM-64
  • Translation:
  • Aliases: n/a
  • Date: c. 1964
  • Frequency: Rare
  • Country: Switzerland
  • Use: Military
  • Refrence: SZ-007
  • Price:? $100+
  • Notes: The SM-64 was most likely used for rescue service and uses a full 'helmet' type design. A rubber outlet valve was molded into the left side of the face piece and a special 40mm filter canister was used. This mask was also issued in pairs to facilitate two masks hooked up to a single breathing unit via two joined breathing tubes (see below). The SM-64 is fairly difficult to find on the surplus market.

  • Type:SM-74

  • Use: Military

  • Price: $50
  • Notes: The SM-74 was for a long time the standard Swiss mask used by the military. The mask uses a simple design with a 40mm thread filter canister. The SM-74 is fairly common on the surplus market but is getting more difficult to find today.

  • Aliases: n/a
  • Date: c. 1981
  • Frequency: Scarce
  • Country: Switzerland
  • Use: Military
  • Refrence: SZ-010
  • Price:? $100+
  • Notes: The Swiss SM-81 is a rare experimental mask that may have been designed for use inside tanks. The mask has a wider field of view compared to the SM-74. The 40mm filter unit is directed downward via a small plastic angle-tube. A standard 5 strap elastic head harness and circular exhale valve is present. The SM-81 is very rare today as few were likely made.

  • Frequency: Uncommon/Rare

  • Use: Military
  • Refrence: SZ-012
  • Price: $225
  • Notes: The SM-90 is the standard Swiss military mask used today. It is manufactured by Huber & Suhner and features many modern updates including a drinking tube, voice amplifier and the ability to use corrective lens inserts. The mask also comes with an adjustable head net for extra comfort. The Swiss SM-90 is somewhat difficult to locate on the surplus market and is typically very expensive when found.

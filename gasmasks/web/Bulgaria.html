  • Refrence: BU-008
  • Price: $125
  • Notes: This mask, which is obviously a direct copy of the US M9, is most likely a Bulgarian made mask that saw use in the Middle-East during the first Gulf War. Interestingly, the mask bears no markings that would definitively indicate the country of origin, leading to some speculation that the mask could actually be Romanian or Yugoslavian. However, the fact that Bulgaria did produce a direct copy of the US M17 (see the PDE-1 below) would be reason enough to speculate that this mask was its predecessor. These M9 copies are certainly rare, and when found are usually in Iraqi carriers very similar to those used with the M74 and M85 series gas masks.

  • Date: c. 1933
  • Frequency: Scarce
  • Country: The Netherlands
  • Use: Military (Mounted)
  • Refrence: NE-001
  • Price:? $200+
  • Notes: The Dutch Model F is definitely a uniquely designed mask that was used for troops on horseback. The face piece was secured via a five point head harness that was attached to a rubber ring around the front of the mask. This method of suspension helped support the weight of the cumbersome breathing tube and filter. Another unique feature is the 'flapper' type exhale valve which was housed inside a metal case fixed in the middle of the breathing tube. The Dutch Model F is quite scarce today and when found it is usually in poor condition.

  • Date: c. 1939
  • Frequency: Rare/Scarce
  • Country: The Netherlands
  • Use: Civilian
  • Refrence: NE-003
  • Price: $200
  • Notes: The Veritex Gas Mask is a civilian protective mask with characteristics similar to several other WWII civilian respirators. Like the German VM44, the Veritex uses a full "helmet" type face piece that covers the whole head and ears. There is also an exhale valve present as part of the inlet assembly. The Veritex mask is made from a thin green rubber material that is prone to dry rot, and thus, few examples survive today in good condition.

  • Type:Model K (Vredestein)
  • Translation:
  • Aliases: K53
  • Date: c. 1950s
  • Frequency: Uncommon
  • Country: The Netherlands
  • Use: Military
  • Refrence: NE-005
  • Price: $40
  • Notes: The Dutch Model K is quite an interesting gas mask. The filter inlet was designed to accept both a 40 mm NATO thread filter canister or a 60 mm thread canister. This was accomplished by a dual threaded inlet assembly. The Model K was originally manufactured by Vredestein, however later an updated version was manufactured by Hevea (see below). The Model K can be found on the surplus market but is becoming more and more difficult to locate.
